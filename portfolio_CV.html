Yoga

I am a state-level yoga player, and my journey in yoga began in my hometown. Initially, my family and I used to practice yoga in our home and a nearby garden. As I progressed, I continued to explore and enhance my skills. During my time at Saraswati Shishu Mandir, I delved into Surya Namaskara, laying the foundation for my yoga practice. As I entered college, my involvement in physical activities expanded to include not only yoga but also Mallakhamb. In my first year, I achieved a significant milestone by winning a gold medal in yoga,

and my team secured the first position in the nodals held at the SISTech Ratigarh campus. Unfortunately, due to the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic and academic commitments, I couldn't participate in the state-level competition. Undeterred by the setbacks, I continued my journey in yoga. In the year 2022, during my third year, my team once again clinched the top spot in the nodals. This time, I had the opportunity to participate in the state-level championship held at Jabalpur Hitkarni College.
Being a gold medalist at the district and nodal levels has been a testament to my dedication and passion for yoga. Each step of my journey has been marked by challenges and triumphs, contributing to my growth as a yoga practitioner and athlete.

Makllakhamb

My Mallakhamb journey began in my college when, initially, I had only heard about it being the state game of our state. It was in my first year when a senior from the third year introduced me to a pole in the sports ground, proclaiming it as the Mallakhamb pole. He showed me a poster of our pass-out senior, Saurabh Arjariya, who was part of the Mallakhamb team that represented India internationally. Seeing his achievements on the wall inspired me to take up the sport. I expressed my interest to my senior, saying I wanted to play Mallakhamb. To my surprise, a few days later, he informed me about an open national Mallakhamb tournament in Chhattisgarh. I was amazed at the opportunity and thought it might be a joke until he took my documents and told me to get ready for the 1st nationals. Even until 1 AM the night before, I thought he was kidding, but he called me and confirmed the reality. I packed hastily, reaching the station without even taking my towel. In Bhopal, I met Saurabh Arjariya, the national player of Mallakhamb, who shared more insights about the sport and asked me to observe. We reached Ambikapur, Chhattisgarh, where I spent three days and learned Mallakhamb for the first time. It was a remarkable experience. Subsequently,

I played Mallakhamb for my college and won gold in nodals. I also participated in the state-level game in Ujjain, where we secured the 2nd position. Unfortunately, none of us were selected for the national team. than I participated in ujjain for open state level mallakhamb championship but there we got another failer , we just learnt from the failers and keep plying and working for mallakhamb .
due to covid-19 our playing is stopped that time but we utilized our time , Saurabh Sir suggested opening a Mallakhamb academy in Vidisha. I formed a team of eight people and registered the "1st Mallakhamb Academy of Vidisha MP," a significant achievement for us.In our persistent efforts to promote Mallakhamb in Vidisha, we reached out to our local government body, the Khel Evam Yuva Kalyan Vibhag, seeking a dedicated place in the stadium for Mallakhamb practice. Despite submitting applications over ten times and repeatedly writing letters to the state secretary, our initial attempts were met with challenges. Our determination did bear some fruit as, after 1 year of struggle and several appeals, we successfully acquired Mallakhamb kits and accessories. However, the quest for a suitable practice space in the stadium remained unfulfilled. The dream of establishing Mallakhamb in Vidisha faced obstacles, but our resolve remains unwavering. We are committed to renewing our efforts, working persistently, and collaborating with local authorities to realize our vision for Mallakhamb in our community. The journey continues, and we are optimistic about achieving our goal with continued dedication and perseverance.
Initially practicing in the college sports hall, we faced challenges due to inadequate facilities. After writing several applications, we received funding to buy mats. However, the procurement was delayed, and we practiced with makeshift arrangements. We organized an open Mallakhamb tournament for schools, selecting 30 players to participate in the state-level tournament in Bhopal. It was a proud moment as we became the 1st team from Vidisha district. In the following year, we went to Ujjain for the state-level open championship, but unfortunately, we were not selected again.
As time passed, I reached my final year, realizing it was my last chance to play at the national level. I participated in the tournament, won gold in nodals, and went to Ujjain for the state-level game. Saurabh Arjariya Sir motivated me with encouraging words, and after the results were announced, I heard my name being selected for the nationals. The All India Inter University Mallakhamb Championship took place in Hanumangarh, Rajasthan, providing a fantastic experience. I had the opportunity to meet renowned players who had received Arjuna Awards and various other accolades. The journey was truly a remarkable and enriching experience.

Finally I got selected for the nationals in my last chance . "DON'T LOSE YOUR HOPES"
